About Me & The Project

My Background

My name is Haithem Chouari, and I am currently pursuing my degree in Agronomy at the Institute of Agronomy and Veterinary Medicine Hassan II (IAV). My studies have ignited a strong interest in modern agricultural practices, particularly in areas like crop health and disease management.

Parallel to my agronomy studies, I've cultivated a passion for programming and technology over the past few years. Starting with fundamental concepts and basic projects in languages like C, Python, and SQL, my interests recently converged with data science and machine learning in 2024. Building upon guided projects and online courses, I'm now focused on applying these skills to real-world problems, leading me to projects like this one.

Project Motivation

The inspiration for PhytoGuardAI stemmed from my exploration of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and their powerful capabilities in image analysis. Recognizing the critical importance of timely and accurate disease identification in agriculture – a core aspect of my agronomy education – I saw an opportunity to apply my newfound machine learning skills. This project became a way to bridge my academic learning in agronomy with my practical development skills, aiming to create a useful tool for farmers and gardeners.

Development Process & Technical Highlights

Developing this application followed a standard machine learning project pipeline:

  • Data Collection: Utilizing a comprehensive dataset from Kaggle containing images of various plant leaves, both healthy and diseased.
  • Preprocessing: Cleaning, augmenting, and preparing the image data to be suitable for model training.
  • Model Selection & Training: Experimenting with different model architectures, including building my own custom models and leveraging powerful pre-trained networks like ResNet and EfficientNet. The model was trained iteratively, focusing on key performance metrics (Accuracy, Recall, F1-score, Precision, Confusion Matrix) to optimize performance.
  • Performance Tuning: Addressing challenges such as model training time by optimizing data loading and treatment functions, and pushing model performance on unseen data through rigorous testing and architectural refinement.
The result is an AI model capable of identifying crop diseases with an accuracy of 99.65% on its training dataset. The front-end is built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, providing a clean and intuitive user interface. The backend, handling the image analysis and prediction, is powered by Python, leveraging frameworks and libraries such as Flask, PyTorch, Scikit-learn, Numpy, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn.

Throughout the development, I also benefited from leveraging AI assistants to refine code and explore solutions, a testament to integrating modern tools into the development workflow.

Future Development & Potential Applications

Looking ahead, I plan to continue improving PhytoGuardAI. Future enhancements could include expanding the range of supported plant types and diseases, providing information on disease severity, suggesting potential treatment options, and further optimizing the model's performance on real-world, diverse images. The long-term vision is to make this technology even more accessible and comprehensive.

This tool has the potential to significantly benefit various users, including:

  • Farmers: Enabling quick and early detection of diseases to prevent crop loss.
  • Hobby Gardeners: Helping them diagnose issues with their plants easily.
  • Agricultural Researchers: Providing a tool for preliminary disease identification.
  • Aspiring Data Scientists: Offering an open-source example of applying ML to a practical problem (code available on GitHub).
